Understand Your Options

Before you start looking at listings, you need to know which program fits your income level:

  • 🏛

    Public Housing Programs

    Owned and managed by local housing authorities, offering subsidized rents for low-income families, the elderly, and persons with disabilities.

    See if you qualify →
  • 🏢

    State-Specific Tax Credit Properties (LIHTC)

    Privately owned apartment complexes that offer lower rental rates in exchange for federal tax credits. These are often newer, high-quality buildings.

    Find LIHTC units near you →
  • 📋

    Income-Restricted Listings

    Apartments set aside by landlords specifically for households making under a certain percentage of the Area Median Income (AMI).

3 Essential Steps to Prepare Your Application

To avoid being placed at the bottom of a long waiting list, successful renters always do these three things beforehand:

  1. Verify Your AMI Bracket

    Housing programs categorize eligibility based on your local Area Median Income (usually 30%, 50%, or 80% of the local average). Knowing your number prevents wasted applications. Calculate yours instantly →

  2. Gather Income Documentation Early

    Have your last 3 months of paystubs, tax returns, and bank statements organized in a single PDF. Speed is everything when a new listing opens up — get alerts when listings open.

  3. Check for Local Preferences

    Many state programs give priority to local residents, veterans, or families with children. Always check if you qualify for a "priority voucher." See priority programs in your area →
